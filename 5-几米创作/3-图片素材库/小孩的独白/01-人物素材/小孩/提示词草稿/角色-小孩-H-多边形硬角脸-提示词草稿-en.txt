Hand-painted watercolor illustration, Jimmy Liao picture book style. Loose ink line outlines — lines with slight tremble, broken strokes, ink density variation. Colored pencil cross-hatching layered over wet-on-wet watercolor washes, watercolor bleeding slightly beyond outlines, paper grain visible, handcrafted brushwork. Plain light cream background with faint watercolor wash and water-edge marks. Character design sheet, three views — front, side, back — arranged side by side.

A boy around 7-8 years old. Slightly large head, thin small body, head-to-body ratio about 1:3.5. Limbs slender, shoulders narrow, weightless-looking.

Face shape (polygon with hard angles): this is not a "drawn" face contour — it's a face that looks like a child drew it themselves. Two barely perceptible corners on each side of the forehead — the ink line made a tiny turn at the hairline edges. A clear angular break at the right cheekbone — not a smooth arc transition, the ink line paused there, turned a corner, then continued downward. Another corner at the left jaw — the ink line visibly changed direction there. The chin is not a smooth curve but an irregular flat base with small corners at each end. The entire facial contour has 5 to 6 pronounced hinge points — not a flowing curve but segments linked one after another. As if the person drawing had no compass and no French curve — just a ruler, drawing segment by segment, the ink lingering a moment longer at each corner, darker than the straight sections. This isn't ugly — it's the texture of being "roughly drawn." Like a child drew a person on paper, and the person came alive.

Features: two very small ink-dot eyes, set nearly one-and-a-half eye-widths apart. The two eyes are not on the same horizontal line — the left eye sits half a hair's breadth higher than the right. Nose a faint ink blob, not centered but slightly to the right. Mouth an extremely faint fine ink line — also not perfectly horizontal, left end slightly higher than right. The whole face's features look like they were placed by hand without measuring — not calculated, just felt.

Hair: dark gray-blue, wet-on-wet watercolor base bleeding irregularly. Bangs of varying lengths, some plastered to the forehead, some sticking up. Crown cowlick off-center, leaning left. A few lines at the back of the head are especially stiff — like they were added as an afterthought.

Clothing: bright orange crew-neck T-shirt, watercolor wash, oversized and loose, collar slightly askew, hem half-tucked. Deep blue shorts, thin white side stripe, one leg slightly rolled. White socks, one slipped to ankle. Red canvas sneakers, one lace loose.

Skin: very faint warm gray-yellow watercolor wash, clean.

Overall: the polygon hard-angle face is the "roughest" — not dirty, not unkempt, but looking like it was drawn by a clumsy hand. Some characters in Jimmy Liao's books are exactly like this — as if the artist, upon reaching this child, referenced nothing and just followed instinct stroke by stroke. This "roughness" is one of the most precious textures in the Jimmy Liao style — not technically rough, but rough in spirit: no refinement, no beautifying, no extra half-millimeter arc to make the face prettier.
