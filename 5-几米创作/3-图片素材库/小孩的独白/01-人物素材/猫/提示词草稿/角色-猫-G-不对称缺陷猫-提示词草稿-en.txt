Hand-painted watercolor illustration, Jimmy Liao picture book style. Loose ink line outlines — lines with slight tremble, broken strokes, ink density variation. Colored pencil cross-hatching layered over wet-on-wet watercolor washes, watercolor bleeding slightly beyond outlines, paper grain visible, handcrafted brushwork. Plain light cream background with faint watercolor wash and water-edge marks. Character design sheet, three views — front, side, back — arranged side by side.

A blue-gray cat — but this cat is asymmetrical. Not drawn crooked, born this way. Left ear stands normally, right ear folded down at the midpoint — not the genetic Scottish Fold kind, but the healed result of an injury from some wandering long ago. A short section of thickened ink line at the fold — that's the scar. Left eye slightly larger than the right — iris color also subtly different, left gray-green leaning green, right gray-green leaning gray. From the front the face reads as slightly tilted — not the jaw, but the ear asymmetry pulls the whole visual weight to the left.

Body: medium build, neither fat nor thin. From the side, one patch of fur on the back grows the wrong way — just right of the spine, a small area where fur grows backward, as if someone petted it against the grain. Tail noticeably shorter than a normal cat — about one-third missing. Not a clean cut but naturally short, the tail bone simply ending at some point, the tip round and blunt, fur there darker than the body.

Fur: short-haired, blue-gray. Because of that patch of reverse fur on the back, the body doesn't read as perfectly smooth — one small act of disobedience. White socks only on three paws — the right hind paw has no white, pure blue-gray, as if the ink ran out.

Overall: every asymmetry on its body says the same thing — this cat is not perfect. But the folded ear, the patch of reversed fur, the unsocked paw — they make this version the most like a real cat. A cat that has lived. Some characters in Jimmy Liao's books are exactly like this — imperfection itself is their face.
