Hand-painted watercolor illustration, Jimmy Liao picture book style. Loose ink line outlines — lines with slight tremble, broken strokes, ink density variation. Colored pencil cross-hatching layered over wet-on-wet watercolor washes, watercolor bleeding slightly beyond outlines, paper grain visible, handcrafted brushwork. Plain light cream background with faint watercolor wash and water-edge marks. Character design sheet, three views — front, side, back — arranged side by side.

An older blue-gray cat — not decrepit, but you can tell it's lived a long time. Body slightly plump but not round — the soft looseness of a slowing metabolism after middle age, not overeating. Side view belly sags slightly, but it's more skin than flesh. Coat overall leaning gray — the blue nearly faded out, just a washed-out gray left behind, like clothes worn for too many years. A few white hairs around the mouth. A small notch on the right ear — not from fighting, more like a scar from scratching too hard when young.

Eyes: half-closed — perpetually half-closed, not sleepy right now. Upper lids slightly drooping, covering the top portion of the irises. Iris color very pale — gray-green faded to gray-white, as if rinsed too many times. The gaze isn't spacing out — it's waiting. It's waited for many things, one more won't matter.

Fur: short-haired, but not as dense as in youth — from the side the fur doesn't lie completely flat, some patches slightly lifting, like the corners of an old rug. One small patch on the back especially pale — not a marking, just age-related uneven fading.

Legs: stout, but different from the fat cat's stoutness — these are old-cat legs with slightly thickened joints. Hind legs angle slightly outward when sitting. The white sock areas are no longer truly white — leaning cream-gray.

Overall: it's seen everything. When the child cries it won't come over — but it won't leave either. It stays where it is, eyes half-closed. That sense of "I know. I'm here."
