An adult blue-gray cat — but this cat's expression is perpetually like it just woke up and hasn't figured out what's going on yet. Body slightly plump, rounded but not heavy. Head on the round side, cheek flesh making the head look like a blue-gray furball.

Eyes: this cat's most defining feature — round eyes, not almond-shaped. Irises gray-green, but because the eyes are large and round, the color appears stretched and paler than other versions. Pupils on the large side — even in normal light they stay slightly dilated, locking the whole face in a state of "currently processing the world but not done yet." Gaze direction always half a beat behind everyone else — when everyone's looking this way it's still looking that way. Not a vision problem — its focus is just always a degree off from everyone else's.

Nose: small triangle, pale pink, the only small patch of warmth on the blue-gray face.

Fur: short-haired, fluffiness between standard and long-haired — not long-haired but not close-fitting either. Should feel like rabbit fur to the touch. Blue-gray leaning gray — like faded denim. A small irregular patch of very pale gray-white on the chest.

Legs: medium length, four white socks — but the sock edges are wavy, as if the hand trembled. Tail medium length, naturally hanging down, tip slightly curled upward.

Overall: a cat you'd expect to stare at the same spot for five minutes straight. Not because it sees something — its brain is just still loading. But its slowness isn't an intelligence problem — it's a philosophical slowness: it's not in a hurry, so it sees more than others. When the child cries it won't come immediately — not indifference, it just hasn't finished looking at the patch of light it was looking at.
