Hand-painted watercolor illustration, Jimmy Liao picture book style. Loose ink line outlines — lines with slight tremble, broken strokes, ink density variation. Colored pencil cross-hatching layered over wet-on-wet watercolor washes, watercolor bleeding slightly beyond outlines, paper grain visible, handcrafted brushwork. Plain light cream background with faint watercolor wash and water-edge marks. Character design sheet, three views — front, side, back — arranged side by side.

An adult blue-gray long-haired cat. Visual volume is twice the actual volume — fur accounts for most of it. Fur does not hang neatly but explodes in all directions — chest fur puffing forward, cheek fur puffing sideways, tail fur bursting into an irregular broom shape. When wind blows, the fur moves in large waves — not a few strands, but the entire contour line shifting.

Face: ringed by fur like an uneven collar. Left cheek fur slightly longer than the right — the face reads as subtly asymmetrical. Ears half-buried in the long fur, ear tips poking out. Small lynx-like tufts at the ear tips.

Eyes: hidden in the fur — not invisible, but the fur's shadow makes the eyes appear deeper set. Almond-shaped, irises gray-green leaning green, the only place with definite color in all the fluffy blue-gray. Expression calm — not lazy, but gentle.

Coat: blue-gray dominant, but the long fur creates layering — surface fur pale gray-blue, undercoat deeper gray. Spine line dark, gradually lightening toward the flanks, tail color somewhere between spine and flank. Broad wet-on-wet watercolor washes are perfect for this cat — the fur's boundaries are naturally blurred.

Legs: also covered in long fur, only white paw tips visible. When walking, paws emerge from and retreat back into the fur.

Overall: should be very soft to hold. Not the fluffy of a breed cat from a pet shop — naturally grown chaos, like a tuft of gray-blue cotton blown by the wind for a long time. When it rubs against you it's not just a touch — it's an entire mass of fur pressing in.
