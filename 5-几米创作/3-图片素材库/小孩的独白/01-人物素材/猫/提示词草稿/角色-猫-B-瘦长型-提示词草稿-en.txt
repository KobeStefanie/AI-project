An adult blue-gray cat — but this one doesn't read as "chubby." Body on the long side, limbs also on the long side — not elegant-long, just slightly off-proportion long. From the side the body is an elongated oval, the spine line subtly undulating within the ink strokes. Belly doesn't droop — the side view shows a tucked abdomen, but not lean-fit, just the flat thinness of not eating much and not caring about food. Head slightly small, half a size too small for the body — the proportions feel subtly wrong for a cat. Ears slightly larger than average, pale pink inside.

Eyes: narrow almond shape, even more slit-like than version A — not an expression, just naturally small eyes. Irises gray-blue leaning gray, nearly blending into the fur color, hard to tell where eye ends and fur begins without looking closely. Gaze unfocused, as if always looking at something far away — even though it's indoors.

Fur: short-haired, close to the body, blue-gray but grayer than version A — less blue, more gray. No clear boundary between spine fur and flank fur, the whole cat one uniform tone, as if covered in a layer of gray mist. Tail thin and long, tip not curled, trailing straight behind.

Legs: slender, white only at the very paw tips — not socks, just four tiny white dots. Hind knee bones slightly prominent in side view.

Overall: a cat that doesn't look like a cat. Sitting there, you might not notice it — it doesn't take up space. But when it looks at you, you stop — not because it's pretty, but because you can't tell what it's thinking.
