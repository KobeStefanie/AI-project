Hand-painted watercolor illustration, Jimmy Liao picture book style. Loose ink line outlines, colored pencil cross-hatching layered over watercolor washes, visible paper grain, wet-on-wet technique. Muted blue-gray with warm amber accents, tiny figure in vast space, large negative space. Handcrafted brushwork.

Same minimal interior. Wooden floor, plain wall, twilight reaching its final stage outside. The child sits on the floor, chin lifted just slightly. Small ink-dot eyes — not confident, but "I need to believe in myself." Lips pressed together. One hand resting on the cat's back. This sentence is spoken to themselves.

A young blue-gray short-haired cat lies beside the child's leg, body pressed against the outer thigh. The cat's eyes are half-closed — not sleepy, just "okay, I heard you." About one year old, neither fat nor thin, normal build.

Outside the window, the warm amber portion is growing — the last warmth of twilight. The child's hand and the cat's back are the most stable point of contact in the frame. The negative space remains, but it no longer feels empty.
