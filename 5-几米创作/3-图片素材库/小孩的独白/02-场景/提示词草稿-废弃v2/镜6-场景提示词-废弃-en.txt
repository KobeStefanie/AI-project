Hand-painted watercolor illustration, Jimmy Liao picture book style. Loose ink line outlines, colored pencil cross-hatching layered over watercolor washes, visible paper grain, wet-on-wet technique. Muted blue-gray with warm amber accents, tiny figure in vast space, large negative space. Handcrafted brushwork.

Same minimal interior. Wooden floor, plain wall, twilight deeper outside. The child sits on the floor, eyes closed or looking down at the floor — hard to tell. Shoulders drawn in, back curved into a soft arc. The whole body shrunk small. The mouth is saying "it's my fault" but every word is being swallowed inward. Not the outward-flinging sulk of shot 2 — this one is sinking in.

A young blue-gray short-haired cat has walked over to the child's leg — the first time the cat has actively approached. The cat's body presses gently against the outer side of the child's calf, head nudging the child's leg — so lightly you'd miss it if you weren't paying attention. About one year old, neither fat nor thin. The blue-gray fur in the twilight is the warmest small patch of color in the frame.

The physical distance between child and cat has closed to zero. The quietest moment in the whole piece. Vast negative space — but something is stirring inside it.
