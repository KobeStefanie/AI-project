Hand-painted watercolor illustration, Jimmy Liao picture book style. Loose ink line outlines, colored pencil cross-hatching layered over watercolor washes, visible paper grain, wet-on-wet technique. Muted blue-gray with warm amber accents, tiny figure in vast space, large negative space. Handcrafted brushwork.

A minimal interior. Wooden floor, a plain wall. Warm gray twilight slants through a window, casting long faint shadows across the floor. A child sits on the floor, facing toward outside the frame — as if speaking to someone at the door. The child has a pear-shaped face — narrow forehead, wider jaw, low center of gravity. Small ink-dot eyes looking up and outward, a thin watery sheen in the eyes but no tears. Mouth slightly open, mid-sentence. Shoulder line tense. A young blue-gray short-haired cat — about one year old, neither fat nor thin, normal build — lies nearby to the side and behind, head lifted, watching the child quietly and calmly.

Mostly blue-gray tones, a thin strip of warm amber by the window. Child and cat are small in the frame, vast negative space around.
