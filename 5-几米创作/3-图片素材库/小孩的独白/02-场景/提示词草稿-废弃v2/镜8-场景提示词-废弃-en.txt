Hand-painted watercolor illustration, Jimmy Liao picture book style. Loose ink line outlines, colored pencil cross-hatching layered over watercolor washes, visible paper grain, wet-on-wet technique. Muted blue-gray with warm amber accents, tiny figure in vast space, large negative space. Handcrafted brushwork.

Same minimal interior. Wooden floor, plain wall. Twilight's final stage outside — warm amber now outweighing blue-gray, but thin and soft, not glaring. The child sits on the floor, gaze directed outside the frame — not defensive, just stating a fact without expecting a response. Pear-shaped face. Small ink-dot eyes calm, the corner of the mouth loosened — not quite a smile, just the line is no longer tight. One hand still resting on the cat. The whole body is an open shape — not sprawled, just no longer drawn in.

A young blue-gray short-haired cat is curled beside the child, eyes closed. The cat's body is a small patch of soft warm gray-blue. The cat doesn't need to listen anymore — it has been here the whole time. Everything the child said, the cat already knew. About one year old, neither fat nor thin, normal build.

Light falls on the floor, the wall, the child, the cat, dyeing the whole corner a quiet warm gray. The negative space remains. But nothing more needs to be said.
